Beyond the agent and the environment, there are key sub-elements that are essential for a reinforcement learning system. Two of these critical sub-elements are the *policy* and the *value function*. **1. Policy** A policy is essentially the agent's decision-making strategy. It dictates how the agent behaves at any given time. More formally, a policy is a mapping from the perceived states of the environment to the actions the agent will take when in those states. Think of it as a set of rules that the agent follows. * **Stimulus-Response Rules:** The policy can be viewed as a set of stimulus-response associations, similar to what you might find in psychology. The "stimulus" in this case is the state of the environment (which can also include internal states), and the "response" is the action taken by the agent. * **Implementation:** The policy can be implemented in various ways. It could be a simple lookup table, where each state has a pre-defined action. Or, it could be a more complex function requiring computation, such as a search process, to determine the best action. * **Core of the Agent:** The policy is the core of a reinforcement learning agent. It's the policy, and the policy alone, that determines the agent's behavior. * **Stochastic Policies:** Policies can be deterministic, meaning that for a given state, the policy always specifies the same action. However, policies can also be stochastic, meaning they specify a probability distribution over possible actions for each state. **2.
